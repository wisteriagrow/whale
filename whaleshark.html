Whale Shark Facts

They Can Process More Than 6,000 Liters Of Water
They draw in big gulps of water, close their mouths, and expel the water gradually through their gills.
They Have Thousands Of Teeth
Though Whale Sharks are not predators like the Great White, they do have small little teeth for when they encounter small fish.
Their Lifespan Is Really Long
The majority of sharks live up to 50 years, but whale sharks can live from 70 to 100 years!
Fingerprint Spots!
Humans have fingerprints that are unique to each person. Likewise, whale sharks' spots are unique to each shark.
Whale Sharks Are Endangered
Because of bycatch and fisheries, Whale Sharks population has greatly decreased and have been declared endangered! Everyone Has The Power To Save These Majestic Creatures.

Whale Sharks are not whales!

But they are the worlds largest fish!

And they are super slow swimmers!
